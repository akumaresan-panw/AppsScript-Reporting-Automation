Welcome to the Slide Deck Automation Guide!

Unlock the power of automation for your business reporting. This comprehensive guide will empower you to streamline repetitive tasks, leveraging Google Apps Script to seamlessly connect Google Sheets data with dynamic Google Slides presentations.

By mastering these steps, you'll not only save invaluable time but also ensure unparalleled accuracy and consistency across all your reports. Dive in and transform your workflow!

Essential Tools for Automation

This powerful workflow is built upon a foundation of key Google tools:

  • Gemini: Your intelligent co-pilot for code generation. With precise prompting, Gemini creates efficient Apps Script code, requiring absolutely no prior technical experience from your side.
  • Google Sheets: The central hub for your data. Design your data templates here, knowing they can be reused consistently for recurring reports, period after period.
  • Google Slides: The canvas for your final, automated presentations. Watch your data transform into polished, professional slides.
  • Apps Script: The magic behind the scenes. This powerful development platform is natively integrated with Google Slides and Sheets. To access Apps Script, navigate to "Extensions" then "Apps Script" within your Google document.

Authorizing Apps Script Projects

Before your Apps Script project can work its magic, it needs permission to interact with your Google files. When setting up a new Apps Script project, you will be prompted to grant authorization. Click "Review permissions" to proceed.

Then, you will be asked to allow access to your Google Account. Click "Continue" to grant the necessary permissions.

Workflow Unveiled: How It All Connects

The automation journey begins once you've identified the recurring slides in your presentations. Your next step is to collaborate with Gemini, prompting it to generate the specific Apps Script code that will power your automation, all based on the templates you define.

CRITICAL TIP: Always instruct Gemini to include an onOpen() function in its code. This vital function ensures that your Apps Script initializes correctly and builds the necessary data pipeline every time your Google Sheet or Slide document is opened.

Crafting Effective Prompts for Gemini

To achieve the most accurate and useful code from Gemini for your automation needs, follow this structured approach to prompting:

  • Select Your Commentary: Pinpoint the exact commentary or text block within your slides that you wish to automate.
  • Identify Dynamic Components: Break down the selected commentary into its changing parts. These typically include:
    • Metrics: Numerical values, percentages, currency amounts, etc.
    • Descriptive Verbs: Words indicating trends like "increased," "decreased," "declined," "remained stable."
    • Categorical Strings: Names of sectors, divisions, companies, etc.
  • Name Your Metrics Clearly: Assign a descriptive, unambiguous name to each dynamic component. The more specific your naming, the better Gemini will understand and integrate it into the code.
  • Create Placeholder Phrases: Develop clear, bracketed placeholder phrases (e.g., [Quarter], [percent of sector of top 10 ea deals]) for each identified changing component within your commentary.

From Commentary to Placeholder: A Transformation

Witness how a standard commentary sentence is transformed into a flexible, automatable template using placeholders:

Original Commentary: Among the top 10 EAs closed in Q3’25 M1+M2: ____% ($____M) of the top 10 EA Deals were from Strategic (___% in PY), followed by Majors ___% ($___M) vs. ___% in PY

Becomes (with powerful placeholders): 

Among the top 10 EAs closed in [Quarter] M1+M2:
[percent of sector of top 10 ea deals]% ($[dollar value of portion of top 10 ea deals]) of the top 10 EA Deals were from [sector] ([percent of prev year]% in PY), followed by [percent of sector of top 10 ea deals]% ($[dollar value of portion of top 10 ea deals]) vs. [percent of prev year]% in PY
[percent]% of the top 10 EA Deals were Repeat ([percent]% in PY) and [percent]% were New ([percent]% in PY)
[STRING company of concern] value capture is below [percent]% due to [STRING reasoning: manual input]

Mastering Placeholder Design: Best Practices

  • Clarity is King: Ensure your placeholder names clearly indicate the meaning of the data they represent.
    • GOOD: Instead of a vague [percent], use [percent of Y/Y total deals that slipped].
    • GOOD: Rather than a generic [value], opt for [largest number of new hires by function].
  • Empower Gemini: Once your placeholders are meticulously defined, feed them into Gemini. The more structured and clear your templates, the more accurately Gemini will generate the Apps Script code you need.

Seamless Data Linking: Google Sheets

For integrating data from BigQuery or other external sources, the =IMPORTRANGE(link, range) function in Google Sheets is your best friend. This powerful function ensures your data automatically updates as source information changes, maintaining a live, referential link without any manual intervention once set up. The `IMPORTRANGE` function is used to bring data into Google Sheets. Its syntax requires the link to the source sheet and the specific range, as demonstrated in the example below.

Key Considerations for IMPORTRANGE:

  • Precise Range Definition: When specifying the range of cells, always include the exact sheet name followed by an exclamation mark and then the cell range (e.g., "Sheet Name!A1:B10").
  • Example: To import cells A10:Q53 from a sheet named “Revenue 2025”: 
    =IMPORTRANGE(“your_sheet_link”, “Revenue 2025!A10:Q53”)

Pro Tips for Optimal Performance:

  • Import Only Essentials: Strive to import the minimum necessary data. Overloading your sheet with excessive data points can significantly impact Apps Script's runtime performance.
  • Efficiency is Key: A lean data import strategy ensures your automation runs swiftly and smoothly.
